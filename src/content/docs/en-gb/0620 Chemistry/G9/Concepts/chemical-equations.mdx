## Word Equations

Magnesium burns in air (reacts with oxygen) to form white solid magnesium oxide.

Word equation: magnesium + oxygen → magnesium oxide.

## Symbol Equations

Magnesium burns in air (reacts with oxygen) to form white solid magnesium oxide.

Symbol equation: $$Mg + O_2 \rightarrow MgO$$

**Important note**  
Diatomic molecules must be written with subscript 2: $O_2$, $N_2$, $H_2$, and halogens $F_2$, $Cl_2$, $Br_2$, $I_2$.

## Balancing Equations: Rules

1. Cannot change subscripts.
2. Can change coefficients (large numbers).
3. Use whole numbers only.

**Balanced example**  
$$2Mg + O_2 \rightarrow 2MgO$$
