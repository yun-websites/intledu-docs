Simple distillation is suitable for separating two or more miscible liquids when their boiling points differ by more than approximately 70°C.

## Key Principle

The liquid with the lower boiling point vaporizes first, rises through the apparatus, condenses in the condenser, and is collected as distillate, while the higher-boiling component remains in the flask.

## Required Apparatus

- Distillation flask
- Anti-bumping granules
- Thermometer (positioned to measure vapor temperature)
- Condenser (water inlet at bottom, outlet at top)
- Receiver adapter
- Beaker or receiving flask
- Tripod, gauze, Bunsen burner, heatproof mat

## Procedure

1. Place the mixture and a few anti-bumping granules into the distillation flask (fill to about one-half full).
2. Assemble the distillation apparatus, ensuring the thermometer bulb is positioned just below the side-arm of the distillation head.
3. Connect the condenser and run cold water through it (in at bottom, out at top).
4. Gently heat the flask using a Bunsen burner on a tripod and gauze.
5. Collect the distillate when the temperature stabilizes near the boiling point of the lower-boiling component.
6. Monitor the temperature throughout collection and record the boiling range.
7. Stop heating once the desired fraction is collected or when the temperature begins to rise significantly.
8. Allow the apparatus to cool before dismantling.

## Important Notes

- Anti-bumping granules provide nucleation sites to ensure smooth boiling and prevent superheating.
- The collected distillate is enriched in the more volatile (lower boiling point) component.
- This method is not effective for separating liquids with close boiling points.
