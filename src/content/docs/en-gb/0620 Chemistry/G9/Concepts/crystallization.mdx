Crystallization is used to recover a soluble solid from its aqueous solution in pure crystal form.

## Required Apparatus

- Evaporating dish
- Tripod and gauze
- Bunsen burner
- Heatproof mat

## Procedure

1. Set up a Bunsen burner with tripod and gauze on a heatproof mat.
2. Pour the solution into an evaporating dish.
3. Gently heat the dish on a blue flame until the solution begins to boil.
4. Continue heating until most of the solvent has evaporated but stop before complete dryness.
5. Allow the dish to cool slowly; crystals usually form on cooling.
6. Carefully collect the solid crystals from the evaporating dish.

**Important**: Avoid heating to complete dryness to prevent decomposition or formation of powdery solid that sticks strongly to the dish.
