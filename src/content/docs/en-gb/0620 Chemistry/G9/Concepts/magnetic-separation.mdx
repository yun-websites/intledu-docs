Magnetic separation is effective when one component of a solid mixture is magnetic and the others are not.

## Procedure

1. Place the mixture of solids into a beaker.
2. Cover a magnet with plastic wrap or a plastic bag.
3. Dip the wrapped magnet into the mixture and stir gently.
4. Slowly withdraw the magnet and observe any particles attracted to it.
5. Gently blow or tap to remove loosely attached non-magnetic material.
6. Repeat if necessary to collect more magnetic substance.

This method requires no heating or complex apparatus and is commonly used in recycling processes (e.g., separating iron from waste).
