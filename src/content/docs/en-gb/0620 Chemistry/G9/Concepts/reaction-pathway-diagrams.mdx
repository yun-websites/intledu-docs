Methane is the simplest hydrocarbon molecule and the main component of natural gas. When it burns, it reacts with oxygen. The products are carbon dioxide and water vapour:

$$ \mathrm{CH_4(g) + 2O_2(g) \rightarrow CO_2(g) + 2H_2O(g)} $$

For an exothermic reaction:

- the energy of the reactants is higher than the energy of the products
- the arrow points downwards to show that energy is given out (released).

The reaction between nitrogen and oxygen is endothermic. It is one of the reactions that take place in the hot engine when fuel is burnt in car engines.

The equation for this reaction is:

$$ \mathrm{N_2(g) + O_2(g) \rightarrow 2NO(g)} $$

For an endothermic reaction:

- the energy of the reactants is lower than the energy of the products
- the arrow points upwards to show that energy is absorbed (taken in).
