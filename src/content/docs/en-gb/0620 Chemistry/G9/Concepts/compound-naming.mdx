All inorganic compounds have a two-part name:  
**Part 1** — positive ion name  
**Part 2** — negative ion name

## Positive Ions

- Simple: name from Periodic Table ($H^+$ is Hydrogen, $Mg^{2+}$ is Magnesium).
- Variable charge: use Roman numerals, e.g., $Fe^{2+}$ is Iron(II), $Fe^{3+}$ is Iron(III).
- Polyatomic: special name, e.g., $(NH_4)^+$ is Ammonium.

## Negative Ions

- Simple (two elements): ends in _-ide_ (oxide, chloride, sulphide).
- Complex (with oxygen): ends in _-ate_ (sulphate, carbonate, nitrate).
- Hydroxide: $OH^-$ is hydroxide.

## Special Cases

$H_2SO_4$ — sulfuric acid  
$HNO_3$ — nitric acid  
$HCl$ — hydrochloric acid  
$H_2O$ — water  
$NH_3$ — ammonia  
$CH_4$ — methane
