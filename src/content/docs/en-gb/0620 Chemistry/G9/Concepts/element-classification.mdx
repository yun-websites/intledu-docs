## Metals

**Core idea: Easily lose electrons → Conductivity + Malleability**

- Mostly solid (except mercury)
- High melting / boiling points
- Hard, dense
- Good conductors (electricity & heat)
- Malleable (hammered into sheets)
- Ductile (drawn into wires)
- Usually grey (exceptions: silver, gold)
- Sonorous

## Non-metals

**Core idea: Tend to gain electrons → Insulation + Brittle**

- Solid or gas (except bromine is liquid)
- Low melting / boiling points
- Low density
- Poor conductors
- Brittle (if solid)
- Not sonorous

## Transition Metals

**Core idea: Complex electron structure → Multiple valences + Catalysis**

- High melting / boiling points
- Hard and dense
- Variable colours
- Variable oxidation states
- Often act as catalysts
