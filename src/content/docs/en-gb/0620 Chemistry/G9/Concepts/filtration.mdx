Filtration is used to separate an insoluble solid (residue) from a liquid (filtrate).

## Required Apparatus

- Beaker
- Filter funnel
- Filter paper
- Conical flask

## Procedure

1. Fold and place filter paper inside the funnel.
2. Place the funnel on top of a conical flask.
3. Pour the mixture slowly from the beaker into the funnel.
4. Wait until all liquid has passed through into the conical flask.
5. Carefully remove the filter paper containing the residue and allow it to dry.
6. (Optional) Transfer filtrate to an evaporating dish for further crystallization.

## Products

- **Residue**: the insoluble solid left on the filter paper
- **Filtrate**: the clear liquid collected in the conical flask

## Advanced Variant

Vacuum filtration — uses reduced pressure to speed up the process significantly.
