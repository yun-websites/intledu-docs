The thermal energy (heat) content of a system is called **Enthalpy (H)** of the system. 

The transfer of thermal energy during a reaction is called the **enthalpy change (ΔH)** (the symbol Δ "delta" means "change in") of the reaction.

## Exothermic Process

- Energy is released to the surroundings.
- ΔH = -x kJ/mol (negative)

## Endothermic Process

- Energy is absorbed from the surroundings.
- ΔH = +x kJ/mol (positive)
