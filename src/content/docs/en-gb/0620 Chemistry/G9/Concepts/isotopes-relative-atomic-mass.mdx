**Definition of Isotope**:  
Atoms of the same element (which have the same atomic/proton number) but have a different mass/nuclear (neutron) number.

**Relative Atomic Mass** ($A_r$) = $\sum$ (mass number × percentage)

## Chlorine Example

| Isotope       | Abundance | Protons | Neutrons | Electrons |
|---------------|-----------|---------|----------|-----------|
| chlorine-35   | 75%       | 17      | 18       | 17        |
| chlorine-37   | 25%       | 17      | 20       | 17        |

$A_r = 35.5$

## Magnesium Example

| Isotope        | Symbol                  | Abundance / % |
|----------------|-------------------------|---------------|
| magnesium-24   | $ ^{24}_{12}\mathrm{Mg} $ | 78.6          |
| magnesium-25   | $ ^{25}_{12}\mathrm{Mg} $ | 10.1          |
| magnesium-26   | $ ^{26}_{12}\mathrm{Mg} $ | 11.3          |

$$ 24 \times 0.786 + 25 \times 0.101 + 26 \times 0.113 = 24.327 $$

Another example:

$$ 6 \times 0.075 + 7 \times 0.975 = 6.975 $$

**Definition of Relative Atomic Mass** ($A_r$) (1 AMU):  
The mass of an element compared to the mass of 1/12 of the mass of carbon-12.
