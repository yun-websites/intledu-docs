This section introduces the principal methods used to separate mixtures in the laboratory. The choice of technique depends on the physical properties of the components (solubility, boiling point, magnetism, etc.).

## Main Separation Methods

- **Filtration**  
  Separates an insoluble solid from a liquid.

- **Crystallization**  
  Obtains a soluble solid from its solution by evaporation and cooling.

- **Magnetic Separation**  
  Separates a magnetic solid from a non-magnetic solid.

- **Simple Distillation**  
  Separates two miscible liquids with a large difference in boiling points (>70°C).

- **Fractional Distillation**  
  Separates two miscible liquids with similar boiling points (\<70°C difference).

- **Paper Chromatography**  
  Separates and identifies components (especially dyes or colored substances) based on differential solubility and adsorption.

## When to Use Each Method

| Mixture Type                        | Recommended Method      | Key Property Exploited       |
| ----------------------------------- | ----------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Insoluble solid + liquid            | Filtration              | Solubility                   |
| Soluble solid dissolved in liquid   | Crystallization         | Solubility & temperature     |
| Magnetic + non-magnetic solids      | Magnetic separation     | Magnetism                    |
| Two liquids (large BP difference)   | Simple distillation     | Boiling point                |
| Two liquids (close BP)              | Fractional distillation | Boiling point + surface area |
| Mixture of dyes / colored compounds | Paper chromatography    | Solubility & adsorption      |
