## Overview

Total internal reflection (TIR) is widely used to guide light efficiently without significant loss of energy.  
It occurs when light travels from a denser medium to a less dense medium at an angle greater than the critical angle.

Common applications include:

- Periscopes
- Optical fibres
- Medical instruments such as endoscopes

## Periscope

A **periscope** allows an observer to see over or around obstacles.

**How it works**

- Light reflects inside the device using mirrors or prisms.
- In prism-based periscopes, **total internal reflection** is used instead of mirrors.
- This allows light to change direction with minimal loss.

**Example uses**

- Submarines
- Observation over walls or around corners

## Optical Fibre

**Optical fibres** are thin strands of glass or plastic used to transmit light.

**Working principle**

- Light enters the fibre.
- It repeatedly reflects along the inner surface.
- The reflections occur due to **total internal reflection**, allowing the light to travel long distances.

## Uses of Optical Fibre

### Medicine

- Used in **endoscopy** to examine internal organs.

### Communication

- Used to transmit **voice and data**.
- Common in **internet and telecommunications networks** because signals travel quickly with very little loss.

## Endoscope

An **endoscope** is a medical instrument used to look inside the human body.

**Structure**

- A bundle of **optical fibres** is used.
- One fibre carries **light into the body** to illuminate the area.
- Another fibre carries the **reflected light back to the observer or camera**.

**Principle**

- Light is guided along the fibres using **total internal reflection**, allowing doctors to see inside the body without major surgery.
