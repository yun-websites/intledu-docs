## Ray Diagram Rules

1. A ray through the centre of the lens does not bend.
2. A ray parallel to the principal axis passes through the focal point (F) after refraction.
3. A ray passing through the focal point (F) emerges parallel to the principal axis (principle of reversibility).

## Real Image (Object beyond 2F)

Characteristics:

- Diminished (smaller)
- Inverted (upside down)
- Real image

**Diagram description:** Object placed beyond the centre of curvature (2F) produces a real, inverted, and diminished image located between F and 2F on the opposite side.

## Virtual Image (Object between F and optical centre)

Characteristics:

- Enlarged (bigger)
- Upright
- Virtual image (not formed by the intersection of real rays)

**Diagram description:** When the object is placed between F and the optical centre, rays diverge after the lens; the virtual image is located by extending the rays backward on the same side as the object.
