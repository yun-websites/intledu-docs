## Gauge Pressure

Gauge pressure is the pressure above atmospheric pressure.

$$ P_g = P_{atm} + \rho g h $$

For example, with water ($ \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 $, $ g = 10 \, \text{m/s}^2 $, $ h = 0.05 \, \text{m} $):

$$ P_g = 100{,}000 + 1000 \times 10 \times 0.05 = 100{,}500 \, \text{Pa} $$

In mmHg units:

$$ P_g = 760 + 50 = 810 \, \text{mmHg} $$

At the same level in a liquid, pressures are equal: $ P_A = P_B $.
