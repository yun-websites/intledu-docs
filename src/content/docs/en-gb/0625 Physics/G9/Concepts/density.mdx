## Density

<br/>
$$
\text{Density} = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Volume}}
$$
<br/>

- Quantity of matter in an object
- Mass measured in kg using a balance
- **SI Unit**: $$ \text{kg/m}^3 $$

### Uses of Density

1. **Flotation**
    - An object less dense than water floats.
    - Applied in making ships.
2. **Sinking**
    - An object more dense than water sinks.

### Mass and Weight

$$
\text{Weight} = \text{Mass} \times \text{Acceleration due to Gravity} \quad (mg)
$$

- On Earth: $ g = 9.8 $ N/kg
- On Moon: $ g = 1.67 $ N/kg

### Sources of Uncertainty (when measuring volume)

1. Water sticks to wall of the measuring cylinder
2. Some water may spill
3. Parallax error
