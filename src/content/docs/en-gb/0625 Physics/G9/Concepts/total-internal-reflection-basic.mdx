## Definition

**Total Internal Reflection (TIR)** occurs when all the light is reflected at the boundary between two media instead of passing through it.

This happens when the **angle of incidence** in the **denser medium** is greater than the **critical angle**.

$$
i > C
$$

---

## Conditions for Total Internal Reflection

Total internal reflection occurs only when **both conditions** are satisfied:

1. Light travels from a **denser medium to a less dense medium**.
2. The **angle of incidence is greater than the critical angle**.

---

## Key Idea

The word **“Total”** means **all the light is reflected**.

- No light is refracted into the second medium.
- The reflected ray has the **same intensity as the incident ray (ideally 100%)**.

This makes total internal reflection very efficient for guiding light, which is why it is used in **optical fibres** and other optical devices.
