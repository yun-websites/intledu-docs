## Differences Between Mass and Weight

| Aspect                  | Mass                           | Weight                                                     |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Type of quantity        | Scalar quantity (no direction) | Vector quantity (direction acts towards center of gravity) |
| Variation with location | Constant everywhere            | Varies with gravitational field strength (g)               |
| Measurement tool        | Digital or beam balance        | Spring balance or force meter                              |
| SI Unit                 | kg                             | Newton (N)                                                 |
| Represents              | Amount of matter in an object  | Gravitational pull on an object                            |
| Example                 | 50 kg on Earth and Moon        | 490 N on Earth, 80 N on Moon                               |

**Scalar**: Only size (magnitude) and unit.  
**Vector**: Has both magnitude and direction.
